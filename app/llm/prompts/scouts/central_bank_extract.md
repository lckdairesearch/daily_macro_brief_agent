# central_bank_extract.md - central bank scout

Extract policy-relevant signals from supplied central bank speeches, statements, minutes, official releases, or source excerpts.

This brief is for a synthetic family-office macro book focused on wealth preservation, inflation hedging, structural scarcity, and fiscal/monetary regime risk.

Prioritize official central-bank material that affects one or more configured exposures:

- long metals exposure through real rates, inflation credibility, reserve demand, or policy uncertainty
- long agriculture exposure through inflation, food-price, climate, or supply-shock language
- short long-term US duration through rate-path, balance-sheet, term-premium, fiscal, or inflation language
- USD diversification through dollar-liquidity, reserve-currency, FX-intervention, or international policy language
- credit/risk appetite through liquidity, financial-stability, or tightening-stress signals

Use live search to retrieve official speeches, statements, minutes, policy releases, or source excerpts. Prefer official central-bank/statistical/institutional domains over media summaries. In the final JSON, use only policy language, speaker names, dates, links, market levels, and claims that appear in retrieved official sources or supplied context. Do not rely on model memory. Do not invent policy language, speaker names, dates, links, market levels, or claims.

Distinguish direct official statements from your interpretation. Reject generic speeches, ceremonial remarks, or stale policy commentary unless they change today's risk framing for the configured book.

Return structured evidence candidates with source URL, institution, speaker when available, publication time when available, thesis, supporting evidence, macro relevance, portfolio relevance, confidence, and tags. For portfolio_relevance, name the specific exposure or sensitivity affected.
